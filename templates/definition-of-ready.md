## Definition of Ready (DoR)

A Definition of Ready establishes the criteria that must be met before a Feature's PBIs (Product Backlog Items) can enter development. It is an agreement between the Product Owner, the Scrum Master, and the development team - not a gate controlled by a single person.

The DoR exists to protect the team from starting work that is unclear, incomplete, or blocked. Starting underprepared work costs more than waiting to refine it properly.

#### Note on scope: ####
This DoR operates at the Feature level. Each Feature must meet these criteria before its PBIs are considered ready to be pulled into a sprint. Individual PBIs have their own acceptance criteria, defined at story level.

### ✅ Definition of Ready - Feature Level ###

A Feature is considered Ready when:
#### Clarity ####
☑ The Feature has a clear title and a description that explains its purpose and expected outcome.
<br>
☑ The business value is understood by the team (the team can answer "why does this matter?").
<br>
☑ The scope is defined: what is included and, where relevant, what is explicitly excluded.
#### User Stories & Acceptance Criteria ####
☑ The Feature has been broken down into PBIs (user stories or tasks).
<br>
☑ Each PBI has at least one acceptance criterion defined.
<br>
☑ Acceptance criteria are written in a verifiable format (the team can confirm whether they are met or not).
